# SEO Link Prospecting

## Trigger

Use when finding evidence-backed pages, sites, organizations, or authors that may legitimately reference a specific asset, then identifying public contact paths and drafting bounded outreach without inventing identities or automating spam.

Do not begin prospecting until the user has a concrete linkable asset or a credible reason another page would reference it.

## Purpose

Produce a small, qualified outreach set tied to real page-level relevance, public contact evidence, and a specific value exchange. Keep discovery, contact research, drafting, sending, tracking, and relationship management as separate actions.

## Inputs

- SEO project context.
- Target page, product, study, guide, tool, template, dataset, opinion, or other linkable asset.
- User domain and approved representation identity.
- Target market, language, geography, and audience.
- Known competitors, publishers, communities, associations, directories, or partners.
- Approved SERP, backlink, browser, search, and public-contact tools.
- Outreach, privacy, compliance, and contact-retention constraints.

## Procedure

1. **Define the reference reason.** State why a specific editor, author, organization, or page would benefit from citing the asset. If the reason is weak, improve the asset before prospecting.
2. **Choose prospect classes.** Identify relevant resource pages, guides, comparisons, statistics pages, directories, associations, local organizations, newsletters, journalists, educators, partners, or pages linking to weaker/outdated alternatives.
3. **Build bounded discovery queries.** Use a small set of topical, asset, competitor, local, and page-type queries. Record search/provider/date/geography.
4. **Inspect page-level relevance.** Keep prospects only when a specific page, article, directory, or editorial context makes the reference plausible.
5. **Use backlink evidence carefully.** Competitor backlink data can reveal patterns, but provider coverage is incomplete and existing links do not prove the same site will link again.
6. **Qualify the prospect.** Assess topical fit, page quality, editorial legitimacy, audience fit, freshness, direct-competitor status, paid-placement likelihood, and the exact missing or outdated element.
7. **Discover public contact paths.** Inspect author pages, editorial guidelines, team pages, contact forms, professional profiles, newsletters, structured data, and visible public email addresses.
8. **Record only observed details.** Store the name, role, address, profile, or form only when it was actually found. Preserve the source URL and collection date.
9. **Minimize personal data.** Do not collect unrelated personal information, private contact details, inferred emails, leaked data, or large contact datasets. Respect removal and retention requirements.
10. **Draft a page-specific angle.** Reference the exact page and reason: updated data, useful tool, missing resource, expert evidence, broken/outdated reference, or legitimate comparison. Do not fabricate familiarity, readership, praise, or prior interaction.
11. **Separate sending from drafting.** Sending messages, enriching contacts, uploading to a CRM, scheduling sequences, tracking opens, or following up requires explicit approval naming the account and scope.
12. **Reject low-quality tactics.** Flag purchased links, undisclosed paid placement, irrelevant directories, mass guest-post schemes, automated comments, spam networks, and manipulative link exchanges.

## Output Contract

Start with:

- linkable asset and strongest reference reason;
- highest-value prospect classes;
- evidence and contact limitations;
- recommended outreach mode.

Then provide:

| Prospect page | Site/organization | Page-level relevance | Evidence source | Public contact path | Suggested angle | Risk/caveat | Priority | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Include a small number of page-specific draft messages only when requested. State explicitly whether any message has been sent.

## Verification

- Every prospect is tied to a specific page or editorial context.
- The reference reason is concrete and truthful.
- Contact details are observed, publicly sourced, dated, and attributed.
- No email, name, title, profile, or relationship was invented.
- Private, leaked, inferred, or unrelated personal data was not collected.
- Direct competitors, paid-placement risks, and low-quality sites are flagged.
- No contact enrichment, CRM write, sequence, message, follow-up, tracking pixel, or publication occurred without explicit approval.
- Drafts are personalized to evidence rather than superficially tokenized mass outreach.

## Failure Modes

- Prospecting without a linkable asset.
- Treating a domain homepage as sufficient page-level relevance.
- Inventing or pattern-guessing email addresses.
- Collecting excessive personal data because it is public.
- Presenting backlink-provider data as complete.
- Scraping or contacting at a scale that violates terms, rate limits, or anti-spam rules.
- Drafting generic praise and fake familiarity.
- Hiding paid placement, sponsorship, or reciprocal-link intent.
- Sending or sequencing outreach during a research-only request.
